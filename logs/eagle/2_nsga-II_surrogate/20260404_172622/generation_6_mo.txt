    - Arguments: ((Target_x, Target_y))
- `train` - Unit will train the provided unit type (only bases and barracks can use this action).
    - Arguments: (Unit_Type)
- `build` - Unit will build the provided building type at the target location, consuming the resource cost from the ally base (only workers can use this action).
    - Arguments: ((Target_x, Target_y), Building_Type)
- `harvest` - Unit will navigate to the target resource, collect a resource and bring it back to the target ally base.
    - Arguments: ((Resource_x, Resource_y), (Ally_Base_x, Ally_Base_y))
- `attack` - Unit will navigate to, and attack the target enemy.
    - Arguments: ((Enemy_x, Enemy_y))
- `idle` - The target unit will do nothing for a round. This is the default for all available units that are not assigned an action.
    - Arguments: ()
REQUIRED JSON FORMAT:
{
  "thinking": "Brief strategy",
  "moves": [
    {
      "raw_move": "(x, y): unit_type action((args))",
      "unit_position": [x, y],
      "unit_type": "worker",
      "action_type": "harvest"
    }
  ]
}
EVERY MOVE OBJECT MUST HAVE ALL 4 FIELDS:
- raw_move: string like "(2, 0): worker harvest((0, 0), (2, 1))"
- unit_position: [x, y] array matching your Ally unit position
- unit_type: "worker", "base", "barracks", "light", "heavy", or "ranged"
- action_type: "move", "harvest", "train", "build", or "attack"

Production policy:
Bases produce workers until resource intake matches production needs for the map size.
Construct a barracks when the base has sufficient resources and none exists in proximity.
Barracks should prioritize producing heavy infantry as primary frontline troops.
Produce ranged infantry when pathing is narrow, terrain is complex, or heavy infantry production is unaffordable.
Prioritize resource generation over excessive unit production:
Resource conversion discipline:
Floating resources for too long is usually a strategic mistake.
Convert resources into workers, barracks, or military units when that creates immediate strategic value.
If the economy is already stable, extra resources should accelerate military pressure.
Harassment policy:
Do not commit all units to a frontal attack; selective disruption can yield more value.
Target isolated workers, threaten key infrastructure, and disengage from costly engagements before taking significant losses.
Sustained interference is valuable when it weakens the enemy economy or delays their army production.
Opening strategy: Aggressive expansion.
Start by rushing basic military units and assigning workers to nonstop gathering of essential resources.
Build a barracks immediately, then rapidly scale up aggressive military production.
This opening excels on large maps or against opponents that do not apply strong early aggression.
Prioritize finishing a low-health enemy when possible, as it can be more efficient in the short term than focusing on other targets.
Focus favorable trades:
Engage when our local force can win or efficiently damage a valuable enemy target.
Avoid taking fights that sacrifice units for little strategic gain.
Worker behavior:
Workers mainly support economy, but one suitable worker should build a barracks as soon as resources allow if none exists.
Do not keep producing extra workers once resource flow is stable and military pressure is needed.
Workers should not remain idle when they could harvest, build, or assist local defense.
TIME-BASED STRATEGY:
Early game (turns 0-100): Focus on harvesting and building a barracks.
Mid game (turns 101-200): Shift toward producing combat units and applying pressure if the enemy is vulnerable.
Late game (turns 201+): Prioritize aggressive attacks on the enemy base and production structures to secure victory.
Movement policy:
Military units should move according to the selected global strategy.
In defensive strategies, stay near the base and nearby resources unless chasing a local threat.
In offensive strategies, advance toward the nearest enemy unit or the enemy base.
Light units move directly toward nearby enemy units.
Heavy units push toward enemy buildings.
Ranged units should keep distance from enemy melee units while staying in attack position.
Global strategy selection:
At every decision step first observe the current game state before choosing actions.
Extract information about worker count, military count, number of bases and barracks, available resources, distance to the nearest enemy unit, distance to the enemy base, distance to the nearest resource, and whether enemy units are near our base.
Choose between four global strategies: Worker Defense, Military Defense, Worker Rush, and Military Rush.
Use defensive strategies when enemy units are close to our base, our army is smaller than the enemy army, or our resources are low.
Use offensive strategies when our army is equal to or larger than the enemy army and we can pressure the enemy effectively.
Prefer worker-based strategies when worker count is low or the economy still needs expansion.
Prefer military-based strategies when worker count is sufficient, a barracks already exists, or sustained military production is needed.
