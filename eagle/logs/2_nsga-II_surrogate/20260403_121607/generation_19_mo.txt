Maintain a stable resource income before expanding military production.
Each base should maintain harvesting workers assigned to nearby resources.
Workers should harvest the closest resource and return it to the closest base.
Avoid sending too many workers to the same resource node.
Production policy:
Bases produce workers until labor supply matches map needs.
Construct a barracks when the base has sufficient resources and none exists nearby, prioritizing Heavy unit production as primary frontline defense.
Produce Ranged units in tight pathing areas with constrained terrain or when Heavy unit production is unaffordable.
Optimize task allocation by prioritizing workers based on their proximity and capacity, allowing them to minimize downtime and maintain a strong defense while still fulfilling other responsibilities.
Target selection:
Light units prefer attacking the closest enemy unit.
Heavy units prefer attacking enemy buildings, especially production structures and bases.
Ranged units should attack from distance and prefer high value or dangerous targets.

When rushing, prioritize the nearest enemy target first unless a high value structure is exposed.
Prioritize chasing enemy units while maintaining core defense and strategic position.
Minimize non-essential unit relocation:

Avoid repetitive movement that does not contribute to core objectives such as expanding resource gathering, defending strategic areas, or accessing key resources. Prioritize meaningful progression towards goals over unnecessary positional adjustments.
Early game strategy:
Establish a stable economy by producing workers and harvesting resources.
Build a barracks as soon as resources allow to enable military production.
