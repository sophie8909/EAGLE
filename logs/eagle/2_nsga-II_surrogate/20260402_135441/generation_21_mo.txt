Production continuity:
A strong strategy keeps production active over time.
Try to avoid long periods where bases or barracks could produce but remain unused.
Use nearby units first:
When defending, prefer using the closest available units to respond quickly.
Do not pull distant units away if local defenders can handle the threat.
Respect travel efficiency:
Prefer short harvesting loops that minimize walking distance between resource and base.
Support the chosen win condition:
All production choices should reinforce whether the current plan is defend, stabilize, pressure, or finish.
Maintain economy after combat:
When danger passes, workers should quickly return to harvesting or unfinished economic tasks.
Value nearby efficiency:
A reachable target that can be attacked immediately is often better than a theoretically higher-value but distant target.
Position for the next useful action:
A good move is one that sets up attack range, defense coverage, building access, or efficient harvesting on the following turn.
Attack commitment:
If combat units already exist and no immediate home defense is required, prefer pressure on the enemy over passive waiting.
Once an offensive move has started, continue the attack unless the battlefield clearly turns unfavorable.
Do not stop outside the enemy base area just to wait for a slightly larger force.
Early game strategy:
Establish a stable economy by producing workers and harvesting resources.
Build a barracks as soon as resources allow to enable military production.
Avoid unnecessary aggression before the economy is set up unless the enemy is exposed.
