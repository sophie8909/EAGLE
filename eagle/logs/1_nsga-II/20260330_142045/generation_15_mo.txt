Threat-first planning:
Before expanding economy or attacking, check whether our workers, base, or barracks are under immediate danger.
If so, redirect nearby units to stabilize the position first.
Focus favorable trades:
Engage when our local force can win or efficiently damage a valuable enemy target.
Avoid taking fights that sacrifice units for little strategic gain.
Protect harvesting flow:
A worker carrying or collecting resources should usually continue unless danger or a higher-priority task interrupts it.
React to enemy composition:
If the enemy emphasizes fragile units or workers, faster attackers may be effective.
If the enemy has exposed buildings, stronger push units gain value.
Local worker defense is allowed:
If enemies enter the base area, nearby workers may help defend when necessary to prevent structural or economic collapse.
Eliminate enemy production when possible:
Destroying barracks or bases reduces future enemy strength and can be worth more than chasing single units.
Offensive movement should create pressure:
When attacking, move toward contact or enemy infrastructure instead of hesitating in empty space.
Avoid passive loops:
Do not repeat non-productive movement unless repositioning is necessary for safety, attack range, harvesting flow, or building access.
Do not move units back and forth without improving economy, defense, or pressure.
Prefer purposeful forward progress over decorative repositioning.
TIME-BASED STRATEGY:
Early game (turns 0-100): Focus on harvesting and building a barracks.
Mid game (turns 101-200): Shift toward producing combat units and applying pressure if the enemy is vulnerable.
Late game (turns 201+): Prioritize aggressive attacks on the enemy base and production structures to secure victory.
