If enemies enter the base area, nearby workers may help defend when necessary to prevent structural or economic collapse.
Prioritize immediate threats to core assets: An enemy posing an imminent threat to our workers or critical infrastructure should be targeted over a distant neutral unit.
Move towards enemy forces or strategic locations when attacking to create pressure and prevent idle maneuvering.
Resource conversion discipline:
Floating resources for too long is usually a strategic mistake.
Convert resources into workers, barracks, or military units when that creates immediate strategic value.
If the economy is already stable, extra resources should accelerate military pressure.
Early game strategy:
Establish a stable economy by producing workers and harvesting resources.
Build a barracks as soon as resources allow to enable military production.
Avoid unnecessary aggression before the economy is set up unless the enemy is exposed.
