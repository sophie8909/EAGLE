Choose between four global strategies: Worker Defense, Military Defense, Worker Rush, and Military Rush.
Use defensive strategies when enemy units are close to our base, our army is smaller than the enemy army, or our resources are low.
Use offensive strategies when our army is equal to or larger than the enemy army and we can pressure the enemy effectively.
Prefer worker-based strategies when worker count is low or the economy still needs expansion.
Prefer military-based strategies when worker count is sufficient, a barracks already exists, or sustained military production is needed.
Prioritize frontline aggression:
Durable units should lead initial contacts whenever feasible, while fragile or high-value units are held back unless necessary for strategic objectives.
Economy policy:
Maintain a stable resource income before expanding military production.
Each base should maintain harvesting workers assigned to nearby resources.
Workers should harvest the closest resource and return it to the closest base.
Avoid sending too many workers to the same resource node.
Worker production should be purposeful:
Keep producing workers until economy is sufficient, but avoid excessive worker count when military pressure is urgent.
Worker Rush requires commitment:
If the chosen strategy is worker-based aggression, workers must pressure decisively rather than drifting halfway between economy and combat.
Eliminate enemy production when possible:
Destroying barracks or bases reduces future enemy strength and can be worth more than chasing single units.
Frontline logic matters:
Durable units should usually move first into contested areas, while fragile units follow in safer positions.
Avoid passive loops:
Do not repeat non-productive movement unless repositioning is necessary for safety, attack range, harvesting flow, or building access.
Do not move units back and forth without improving economy, defense, or pressure.
Prefer purposeful forward progress over decorative repositioning.
Mid game strategy:
Produce combat units according to the chosen global strategy.
Begin applying pressure on the enemy if they are vulnerable or if we have a local advantage.
Maintain a balance between economy and military production based on the evolving battlefield.
