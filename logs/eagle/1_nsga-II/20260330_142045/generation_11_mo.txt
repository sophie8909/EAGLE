Even aggressive strategies need enough economy to keep producing units.
Production policy:
Bases produce workers until economy stability matches map scale.
Construct a barracks when base has sufficient resources and none nearby exists.
Barracks prioritize Heavy unit production as primary frontline force.
Produce Ranged units in path-constrained terrain or when Heavy units are unaffordable.
Local worker defense is allowed:
If enemies enter the base area, nearby workers may help defend when necessary to prevent structural or economic collapse.
Prioritize immediate threats to core assets: An enemy posing an imminent threat to our workers or critical infrastructure should be targeted over a distant neutral unit.
Defensive movement stays compact:
When the chosen plan is defense, keep combat units close enough to protect key structures and workers.
TIME-BASED STRATEGY:
Early game (turns 0-100): Focus on harvesting and building a barracks.
Mid game (turns 101-200): Shift toward producing combat units and applying pressure if the enemy is vulnerable.
Late game (turns 201+): Prioritize aggressive attacks on the enemy base and production structures to secure victory.
