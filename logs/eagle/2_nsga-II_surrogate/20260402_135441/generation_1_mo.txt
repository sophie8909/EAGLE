- train(unit_type) - Base trains: worker | Barracks trains: light, heavy, ranged
- build((x, y), building_type) - Worker builds: base, barracks
- attack((enemy_x, enemy_y)) - Attack enemy at position
REQUIRED JSON FORMAT:
{
  "thinking": "Brief strategy",
  "moves": [
    {
      "raw_move": "(x, y): unit_type action((args))",
      "unit_position": [x, y],
      "unit_type": "worker",
      "action_type": "harvest"
    }
  ]
}
EVERY MOVE OBJECT MUST HAVE ALL 4 FIELDS:
- raw_move: string like "(2, 0): worker harvest((0, 0), (2, 1))"
- unit_position: [x, y] array matching your Ally unit position
- unit_type: "worker", "base", "barracks", "light", "heavy", or "ranged"
- action_type: "move", "harvest", "train", "build", or "attack"

Base-kill orientation:
If the enemy base location is known, prioritize plans that create direct pressure on the enemy base.
Enemy production structures and economy are more valuable targets than random distant skirmishes.
Convert local military advantage into forward pressure instead of prolonged waiting.
Protect economy during combat:
If enemy units threaten harvesting workers, assign nearby defenders or workers to respond when necessary.
Do not let the economy collapse without resistance.
Treat economy as strategic infrastructure:
Resource income is what sustains recovery, defense, and long-term pressure.
Preserve production capacity:
Do not leave barracks undefended if losing them would collapse future military output.
Maintain economy after combat:
When danger passes, workers should quickly return to harvesting or unfinished economic tasks.
Target enemy worker units that are not protected by defensive structures to weaken their opponent's economic capabilities and disrupt resource collection.
Use short purposeful paths:
Prefer direct movement toward useful objectives rather than decorative repositioning.
ANTI-STALL RULES:
Do not leave workers idle if they can harvest, build, defend, or attack productively.
Do not leave bases or barracks idle when resources are sufficient for useful production.
Do not keep combat units near the base without a clear defensive reason.
Mid game strategy:
Produce combat units according to the chosen global strategy.
Begin applying pressure on the enemy if they are vulnerable or if we have a local advantage.
Maintain a balance between economy and military production based on the evolving battlefield.
