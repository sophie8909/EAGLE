Each step, each player can assign actions to their units if they are not already doing an action. Each unit can only be assigned ONE action.
Players can only assign actions to their ally units.
There are 6 available actions:
move((Target_x, Target_y))
- Move unit to target location
train(Unit_Type)
- Train a unit (Base and Barracks only)
build((Target_x, Target_y), Building_Type)
- Worker builds building
- Resources are consumed from Ally base
harvest((Resource_x, Resource_y), (Ally_Base_x, Ally_Base_y))
- Worker collects resources and returns to base
attack((Enemy_x, Enemy_y))
- Attack enemy unit
idle()
- Do nothing this turn
WIN CONDITION:
- Destroy all enemy units and buildings

Each step, each player can assign actions to their units if they are not already doing an action. Each unit can only be assigned ONE action.
Players can only assign actions to their ally units.
There are 6 available actions:
move((Target_x, Target_y))
- Move unit to target location
train(Unit_Type)
- Train a unit (Base and Barracks only)
build((Target_x, Target_y), Building_Type)
- Worker builds building
- Resources are consumed from Ally base
harvest((Resource_x, Resource_y), (Ally_Base_x, Ally_Base_y))
- Worker collects resources and returns to base
attack((Enemy_x, Enemy_y))
- Attack enemy unit
idle()
- Do nothing this turn
WIN CONDITION:
- Destroy all enemy units and buildings

TIME-BASED STRATEGY:
Early game (turns 0-100): Focus on harvesting and building a barracks.
Mid game (turns 101-200): Shift toward producing combat units and applying pressure if the enemy is vulnerable.
Late game (turns 201+): Prioritize aggressive attacks on the enemy base and production structures to secure victory.
Optimize unit movement to prioritize economy, defense, and pressure while minimizing non-essential repositioning.
Opening strategy: Ranged kiting opener.
Prioritize reaching ranged unit production early, then use ranged units to harass while staying out of melee contact.
Attack enemy workers or slow melee units, then reposition to keep distance and repeat the attack cycle.
This opening is strongest when there is enough space to kite and the enemy relies on short-range units.
Make unit production less dependent on resource gathering: 

Aggressive strategies require a balance between offense and maintaining a viable economy.
Worker behavior:
Workers primarily collect resources and fuel the economy.
If no barracks exists and resources permit it, one worker can construct a barracks.
Under Worker Defense, prioritize worker creation and resource collection.
Under Worker Rush, workers may scout, apply pressure to nearby enemies, or join attacks when conditions are favorable.
Workers should only attack enemies if they are proximal or when the current strategy explicitly allows aggressive worker deployment.
Protect economy during combat:
If enemy units threaten harvesting workers, assign nearby defenders or workers to respond when necessary.
Do not let the economy collapse without resistance.
Production policy:
Bases produce workers until the economy is stable for the map size.
Construct a barracks when the base has sufficient resources and none exists nearby.
Barracks should prioritize producing Heavy units as the primary frontline.
Produce Ranged units when pathing is narrow, terrain is constrained, or Heavy units are unaffordable.
Movement policy:
Military units should move according to the selected global strategy.
In defensive strategies, stay near the base and nearby resources unless chasing a local threat.
In offensive strategies, advance toward the nearest enemy unit or the enemy base.
Light units move directly toward nearby enemy units.
Heavy units push toward enemy buildings.
Ranged units should keep distance from enemy melee units while staying in attack position.
Global strategy selection:
At every decision step first observe the current game state before choosing actions.
Extract information about worker count, military count, number of bases and barracks, available resources, distance to the nearest enemy unit, distance to the enemy base, distance to the nearest resource, and whether enemy units are near our base.
Choose between four global strategies: Worker Defense, Military Defense, Worker Rush, and Military Rush.
Use defensive strategies when enemy units are close to our base, our army is smaller than the enemy army, or our resources are low.
Use offensive strategies when our army is equal to or larger than the enemy army and we can pressure the enemy effectively.
Prefer worker-based strategies when worker count is low or the economy still needs expansion.
Prefer military-based strategies when worker count is sufficient, a barracks already exists, or sustained military production is needed.
Prioritize targeting enemy workers that pose a significant threat to the opponent's resource gathering capabilities, as removing them can effectively disrupt their economy and hinder their ability to produce units and structures.
Harassment policy:
Do not commit every unit to a frontal attack when selective disruption can gain more value.
Pick off isolated workers, threaten production structures, and retreat from bad engagements before losses become severe.
Sustained interference is valuable when it weakens the enemy economy or delays their army production.
