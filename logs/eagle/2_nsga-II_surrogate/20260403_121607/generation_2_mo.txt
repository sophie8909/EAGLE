Workers should harvest the closest resource and return it to the closest base.
Avoid sending too many workers to the same resource node.
Production policy:
Bases produce workers until the economy is stable for the map size.
Construct a barracks when the base has sufficient resources and none exists nearby.
Barracks should prioritize producing Heavy units as the primary frontline.
Produce Ranged units when pathing is narrow, terrain is constrained, or Heavy units are unaffordable.
Workers are valuable economic units:
Do not throw them into combat unless the tradeoff is strategically justified.
Target selection:
Light units prefer attacking the closest enemy unit.
Heavy units prefer attacking enemy buildings, especially production structures and bases.
Ranged units should attack from distance and prefer high value or dangerous targets.
When defending, prioritize enemies threatening our base, barracks, or workers.
When rushing, prioritize the nearest enemy target first unless a high value structure is exposed.
Avoid idle drifting:
Units should not wander without purpose.
Movement should support defense, regrouping, harvesting, construction, or attack.
Avoid passive loops:
Do not repeat non-productive movement unless repositioning is necessary for safety, attack range, harvesting flow, or building access.
Do not move units back and forth without improving economy, defense, or pressure.
Prefer purposeful forward progress over decorative repositioning.
Late game strategy:
Focus on aggressive attacks to destroy the enemy base and production structures.
Do not hesitate to commit to pressure if it can lead to victory, even if it risks some losses.
