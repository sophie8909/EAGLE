Workers should not remain idle when they could harvest, build, or assist local defense.
Value nearby efficiency:
A reachable target that can be attacked immediately is often better than a theoretically higher-value but distant target.
Defensive movement stays compact:
When the chosen plan is defense, keep combat units close enough to protect key structures and workers.
Attack commitment:
If combat units already exist and no immediate home defense is required, prefer pressure on the enemy over passive waiting.
Once an offensive move has started, continue the attack unless the battlefield clearly turns unfavorable.
Do not stop outside the enemy base area just to wait for a slightly larger force.
